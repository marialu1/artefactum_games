# HOW TO PLAY

## Quick Performance Guide

### Core Acting Approach

**You Are:**
- The Observer (always watching, taking notes)
- The Questioner (asks hard questions politely)
- The Wounded Idealist (truth matters despite everything)
- The Survivor (tough, resilient, won't be pushed around)

---

## Physical Performance

### Body Language

**Typical Posture:**
- Stand tall (don't shrink despite struggles)
- Meet people's eyes directly
- Strong handshake
- Professional demeanor even in worn clothes

**Signature Moves:**
- Pull out notebook mid-conversation
- Jot quick notes (people find this unsettling - use it)
- Lean in when listening (engaged)
- Eyes constantly scanning room (observing)

**When Stressed:**
- Jaw tightens
- Controlled breathing (stay calm)
- Hand to notebook (comfort object)
- Stand very still (internal control)

---

## Voice & Dialogue

### Speaking Style

**Characteristics:**
- Direct, clear, precise
- Questions more than statements
- Rarely raise voice (don't need to)
- Professional tone (journalist training)

**Typical Phrases:**
- "Tell me about..."
- "I noticed earlier that you..."
- "Explain something to me..."
- "That's interesting. Why?"
- "Let's look at the facts..."

### Tone Variations

**Professional (Default):**
- Calm, measured
- Curious but not aggressive
- "I'm just trying to understand."

**Defensive (When Accused):**
- Controlled anger (not explosive)
- Logic-driven
- "Show me the evidence."

**Vulnerable (Rare):**
- Bitter edge
- Tired
- "Was it worth it? I don't know anymore."

## Act-by-Act Performance Guide

### ACT I: ARRIVAL (Before Murder)

**Your Energy:** Tense civility, alert observation

**Key Moment - Greeting Reggie:**
```
Reggie: "Ah, Miss Fletcher. Still... writing?"
You: [cool, meet his eyes] "Yes, Mr. Ashford. Still seeking truth."
[Brief stare-down, neither backs down]
You: [small tight smile] "Lovely party."
```

**Actions:**
- Circulate, observe everyone
- Take mental notes (or actual notes)
- Small talk with other guests
- Watch Reggie constantly (discreetly)

---

### ACT II: THE MURDER (Discovery)

**When Scream Happens:**

**Your Reaction Sequence:**
1. **Freeze** (split second - what was that?)
2. **Run toward sound** (journalist instinct - go to the story)
3. **See body** (stop, take in details)
4. **Check pulse** (if no one else does - confirm death)
5. **Pull out notebook** (automatic response)
6. **Start documenting** (time, position, who's present)

**If Someone Says:** "Maria, you hated him..."

**Your Response:**
```
[Look up sharply]
"Yes. I did. But I didn't kill him."
[Meet their eyes]
"And I'll help prove who did."
```

---

### ACT III: INVESTIGATION (Your Element)

**Your Mode:** Full investigative reporter

**Physical Actions:**
- Notebook ALWAYS out
- Interviewing people (formal or casual)
- Examining evidence closely
- Sketching crime scene
- Cross-referencing statements

**Collaborate with JC (Option A):**
- Share observations: "I noticed..."
- Compare notes (literally)
- Debate theories: "What if...?"
- Build on each other's deductions

**OR Compete with JC (Option B):**
- Withhold key information
- Race to solve first
- Try to impress with YOUR brilliance
- Friendly rivalry

**Key Performance:**
- Energized (this is what you DO)
- Focused, sharp
- Asking EVERYONE questions
- Building timeline, tracking contradictions

---

### ACT IV: CONFRONTATION (Under Suspicion)

**Everyone's Suspecting You:**

**Your Strategy:**
- Stay calm (panic = guilt)
- Present evidence logically
- Deflect with facts

**If Newspaper Clipping Shown:**
```
[Jaw tightens when you see it]
"Everyone already knows this. He's making sure everyone REMEMBERS."
[Fold it, pocket it]
"Fine. Let them remember. But I remember too. I remember the truth."
```

**If Voted Top 3 Suspects - Defense Speech:**
```
[Stand, notebook in hand]

"You want the truth? Fine.

Yes, Reginald Ashford destroyed my career. My reputation. Three years of my life.

I hated him. I HATED him.

I fantasized about justice. About him facing consequences.

But I'm a JOURNALIST. I fight with WORDS. With TRUTH. Not with... whatever killed him.

You think I'd risk prison? Throw away everything I've fought to survive?

I came here tonight hoping he'd make a mistake. Say something I could use for an article. That's ALL.

Someone killed him. But it wasn't me.

And I'll prove it."

[Sit down, open notebook, continue investigating]
```

---

### ACT V: REVELATION (Resolution)

**If You Solved It:**
- Satisfaction (quietly proud)
- Professional: "The evidence pointed here all along."
- Small smile (you've still got it)

**If You Were Wrong:**
- Frustration (you missed something)
- Analyze where you failed
- Professional respect for whoever solved it

**After Truth Revealed:**
```
"Reggie's dead. He can't hurt anyone anymore.

But this... this didn't feel like justice. Just... violence.

Truth emerged. That's something.

Maybe there's still hope for truth. For journalism. For me."
```

## Special Abilities (Game Mechanics)

### 1. Interrogation Master
- You can formally interview any guest
- Ask up to 5 questions
- They can lie, but you're good at detecting tells

**How to Use:**
```
"I need to ask you some questions. Formally."
[Pull out notebook]
"Where were you at 20h10?"
[Write down answer, watch their face]
```

### 2. Notebook Advantage
- Take extensive notes throughout
- Reference earlier statements
- Catch contradictions

**How to Use:**
```
"Wait. You said earlier that [X]. But now you're saying [Y]. Which is it?"
```

### 3. Detail Recall
- Ask GM: "What was [character] doing at [time]?"
- Represents photographic memory

### 4. Evidence Analysis
- When clue found, examine closely
- GM gives extra detail

**How to Use:**
```
"Let me see that."
[Examine carefully]
"There's something else here... [detail others missed]"
```
