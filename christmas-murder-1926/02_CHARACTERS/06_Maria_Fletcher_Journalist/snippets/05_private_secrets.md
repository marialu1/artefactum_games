# PRIVATE SECRETS

## What You Hide From Everyone

### Secret #1: The Dark Fantasies

**You've Fantasized About Reggie's Death.**

For three years, you've imagined scenarios:
- Heart attack during business meeting
- Hit by automobile
- Exposed so thoroughly he kills himself
- Public humiliation that destroys him completely

**You never planned to act on these fantasies.**

But you've thought about them. Often.

**Tonight, when he dies:**
- Part of you feels RELIEF (Good. He's dead. He can't hurt anyone anymore.)
- Part of you feels GUILT (I shouldn't feel this way. I'm not a monster.)
- Part of you feels FEAR (Everyone will think I did this.)

**This secret makes you vulnerable:**
- If anyone knew you fantasized about his death, they'd be certain you killed him
- The line between thinking and doing feels uncomfortably thin tonight
- You have to be VERY careful not to show satisfaction at his death

### Secret #4: The Alternative Career Path You Almost Took

**March 1924: You Almost Gave Up.**

After Herald fired you, blacklisted, drowning in debt:
- Thought about becoming teacher (like mother)
- Thought about leaving New York entirely
- Thought about quitting journalism forever

**What stopped you:**
- Stubborn pride ("He doesn't get to WIN")
- Anger ("I was RIGHT. Truth matters.")
- Can't imagine life without investigating

**But some days you wonder:**
- Was it worth it?
- Should you have just stayed quiet?
- Would you be happy now if you'd chosen differently?

**Tonight, these doubts resurface:**
- Especially if accused of murder
- "I sacrificed everything for truth. And for what?"

**You hated Reggie Ashford.**

**You fantasized about his death.**

**You obsessively documented his life.**

**BUT YOU DIDN'T KILL HIM.**

## Playing These Secrets

**Don't reveal unless forced to.**

**Show internal conflict through:**
- Brief hesitations
- Micro-expressions of guilt/relief
- Defensive reactions when pressed
- Careful word choices

**Example:**

Player: "Did you want him dead?"

You: [pause, too long] "I... wanted justice."

Player: "That's not an answer."

You: [meeting their eyes] "I wanted him to face consequences. Yes."

[Not quite a lie. Not quite the truth.]
