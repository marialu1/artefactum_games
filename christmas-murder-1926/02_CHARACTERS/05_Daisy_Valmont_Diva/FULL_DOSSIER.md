## CHARACTER ESSENCE

### **Who Is Daisy Vermont?**

**In Three Sentences:**

Daisy Vermont is a **stunning 28-year-old cabaret singer** at Harlem's Cotton Club, known as "The Scarlet Nightingale" for her voice that "makes men weep." Beneath her **theatrical, dramatic exterior** lies a woman on a **secret mission**: she seduced Reggie Ashford not for love, but to **infiltrate his life and investigate her half-sister Eleanor's suspicious death** three years ago. Though she's **INNOCENT of Reggie's murder**, her affair with him (ended cruelly two months ago) makes her the **most obvious suspect** - a crime passional red herring wrapped in feathers and sequins.

### **Core Paradox**

**"Performance as Survival"**

Everything is theater - tears, laughter, seduction, grief. BUT beneath the performance is REAL pain (abandoned as child, half-sister dead, rejected by Reggie). Question: Where does the act end and real emotion begin? Does SHE even know anymore?
