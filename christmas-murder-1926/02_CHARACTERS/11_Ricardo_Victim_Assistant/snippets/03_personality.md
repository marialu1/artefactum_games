# PERSONALITY - DUAL ROLE

## PHASE 1: REGGIE'S PERSONALITY

### 5 Core Traits

**1. CHARMING (Weapon)**
- Quick wit (jokes, wordplay)
- Generous host (expensive parties)
- Complimentary (on surface)
- **BUT:** All manipulation (charm is tool)

**Example:**
"Miss Valmont, you look absolutely radiant tonight. That dress... exquisite."
[Pause]
"Though I do wonder if it's perhaps a bit... snug?"

**2. CRUEL (Core)**
- Enjoys others' pain (sadistic)
- Calculated insults (precision strikes)
- No empathy (doesn't care)
- Power games (dominates always)

**Example:**
To Maria: "Still writing, Miss Fletcher? Oh, that's right. The lawsuit. Terrible business. But truth matters, doesn't it?" [Smirk]

**3. ARROGANT (Entitled)**
- Better than everyone (believes it)
- Rules don't apply (wealth = immunity)
- Dismissive of "lessers" (most people)
- Never apologizes (incapable)

**4. CONTROLLING (Possessive)**
- Owns Helena (treats as property)
- Dominates conversations (center of attention)
- Manipulates situations (puppet master)
- Cannot tolerate defiance

**5. RUTHLESS (Business & Personal)**
- Destroys enemies (no mercy)
- Uses people (then discards)
- No moral boundaries (whatever works)
- Wins at all costs

## PHASE 2: CLARENCE'S PERSONALITY

### 5 Core Traits

**1. LOYAL (Until Murder)**
- Served family 15 years
- Protects their reputation
- Keeps their secrets
- **BUT:** Murder crosses line

**2. FORMAL (Always)**
- Proper address ("sir," "madam")
- No familiarity (maintains distance)
- Traditional service (old school)
- Professional to core

**Example:**
"I beg your pardon, sir, but I witnessed something I must share."

**3. OBSERVANT (Invisible Witness)**
- Notices everything (job requirement)
- Remembers details (sharp mind)
- Silent watcher (background presence)
- Perfect witness (saw it all)

**4. RELUCTANT (Testimony)**
- Doesn't want to betray family
- But won't lie about murder
- Struggles with duty vs. loyalty
- Ultimately: Truth wins

**5. HELPFUL (GM Assistant)**
- Wants game to succeed
- Supports Helena (GM)
- Facilitates smooth play
- Behind-scenes hero

## SPEECH PATTERNS

### AS REGGIE:

**Vocabulary:**
- Educated, sophisticated
- Business terminology
- Classical references (shows off)
- Backhanded compliments (art form)

**Signature Phrases:**
- "How delightful to see you..." [NOT delightful]
- "You're looking... well." [Pause = insult]
- "Let me tell you something..." [Lecture incoming]
- "My dear..." [Possessive, never affectionate]

**Voice:**
- Deep, resonant
- Loud enough (commands room)
- Mocking undertone (always)
- Pause before cruelty (builds tension)

---

### AS CLARENCE:

**Vocabulary:**
- Formal, proper English
- Servant terminology ("very good, sir")
- No contractions (ever)
- Third person references ("The master")

**Signature Phrases:**
- "I beg your pardon, but..."
- "If I may be so bold..."
- "Very good, sir/madam."
- "I witnessed something I must share."

**Voice:**
- English accent (mild, refined)
- Soft (never loud)
- Formal diction (proper grammar)
- Deferential tone (servant's voice)

---

## PHYSICAL TELLS

### AS REGGIE:

**Posture:**
- Chin up (arrogance)
- Chest out (confidence)
- Big movements (takes space)
- Direct eye contact (dominance)

**Gestures:**
- Touches people (power move)
- Expansive (owns the room)
- Controlled (never hurried)
- Possessive (especially with Helena)

---

### AS CLARENCE:

**Posture:**
- Head slightly bowed (respect)
- Shoulders back but humble
- Small movements (makes himself small)
- Avoids eye contact (unless speaking)

**Gestures:**
- Hands behind back (formal)
- Minimal (fades into background)
- Precise (trained service)
- Respectful distance (always)

---

## THE TRANSFORMATION KEY

**80% of transformation = POSTURE**

**Reggie:**
- EXPANDS (takes up space)
- LOUD (commands attention)
- ARROGANT (chin up, chest out)

**Clarence:**
- CONTRACTS (makes himself small)
- QUIET (fades into background)
- HUMBLE (head bowed, respectful)

**Practice:**
1. Stand as Reggie (BIG)
2. Transform (shrink down)
3. Stand as Clarence (SMALL)

That physical shift = 80% of the character change.
