## CHARACTER ESSENCE

### **Who Is John Alfred Jones?**

**In Three Sentences:**

Jo√£o Carlos "JC" Mendes is a **45-year-old former NYPD detective** who spent 20 years solving murders in Manhattan before retiring after a corruption scandal forced him out. He's a **Portuguese immigrant** who came to America at age 15 seeking opportunity, bringing with him a humble demeanor and relentless work ethic that made him one of the NYPD's best investigators - though you'd never know it from his self-deprecating manner and rumpled appearance. **Hired mysteriously by Helena Ashford** just weeks before her Christmas Eve party "for unspecified security," JC finds himself investigating the **murder of his own employer's husband** - making him both investigator AND suspect in a case that will test every skill he's learned.

He learned early that people confess more to someone they underestimate. The paradox: his greatest strength (intelligence) is hidden by his greatest weapon (humility).

**April 1921:**
- Murder at speakeasy (Lower East Side)
- Victim: Tommy Chen (Chinese immigrant, bartender)
- Initial ruling: "Robbery gone wrong"
- **JC's investigation:** Discovered it was execution (police-sanctioned)

**What JC Uncovered:**

Tommy Chen witnessed:
- Police captain accepting $5,000 bribe

- Witness statements (Chen told friends about bribe)

**The Choice:**

**Option A:** Report evidence (truth, justice, duty)
- **Result:** Captain arrested, corruption exposed
- **Cost:** JC's career destroyed, possibly killed

**Option B:** Bury evidence (survival, career, compliance)
- **Result:** Captain protected, corruption continues
- **Cost:** JC's integrity, innocent man's death ignored

**JC Chose A.**

**What Happened:**

**JC reported evidence** to Internal Affairs:

**Phase 1: Observation (20h18-20h30)**

- Blood spots (trail from office to ballroom)

**The People:**
- Helena's scream (genuine horror OR performance?)
- James's controlled reaction (too calm for friend?)
- Emilia's confusion (innocent?)
- Others' various reactions (note everything)

**Phase 2: Initial Interviews (20h30-21h00)**

**Strategy:**
- Talk to everyone briefly (establish timelines)
- Non-threatening questions ("Where were you?")

- "What time did you arrive?"
- "Where were you between 8:10 and 8:18?"
- "When did you last see Mr. Ashford alive?"
- "Did you notice anything unusual?"
- "How well did you know the deceased?"

**Phase 3: Evidence Gathering (21h00-21h30)**

- Cross-reference (who saw what when)

**Phase 4: Focused Interrogation (21h30-22h00)**

**Target Prime Suspects:**
- James (financial motive + access)

- Others with strong motives (Daisy, twins, etc.)

**Techniques:**
- **Columbo Turn:** "Just one more thing..."
- **Sympathetic ear:** "I understand why someone would want him dead..."
- **Timeline trap:** "You said you were X, but Y saw you at Z..."
- **Evidence reveal:** Show wire, documents, catch reactions

**Phase 5: The Confrontation (22h00-22h30)**

**When He Has Enough:**
- Call everyone together
- Present evidence (methodical, clear)
- Identify killers (James + Helena)
- Explain how (timeline, motive, method)
